# 03 — ICP: The Kuwaiti Woman We Sell To

> Every section, every headline, every CTA on this site must be written for **one specific person**. If a copy line doesn't speak to her, it doesn't ship.

## Primary persona — "Noura"

| Trait | Detail |
|---|---|
| Name | نورة (Noura) |
| Age | 24–42 |
| Location | Kuwait (Hawalli / Salmiya / Jahra / Farwaniya / Kuwait City) |
| Marital status | ~55% married, ~30% single, ~15% divorced |
| Income | Personal disposable income 200–800 KWD/month, household 1,500–4,000 KWD/month |
| Devices | iPhone (60%), Samsung high-end (35%), other (5%). Almost 100% mobile. |
| Apps where she lives | Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok, WhatsApp |
| Languages | Arabic (Kuwaiti) primary, English secondary |
| Education | High school → University |
| Identity | Modern but values family + tradition; appearance matters socially (family gatherings, weddings, gym, work); strong "what will people say" pressure but also strong "I deserve this" mentality. |

## What she actually wants (deep desires, not surface)

Surface wants → Deep desires:

1. **"بشرة صافية"** (clear skin) → To feel **seen and admired** at the next family gathering / wedding without being asked "تعبانة؟" (are you tired?).
2. **"شعر طويل ولامع"** (long shiny hair) → To feel **feminine and desirable** for her husband / future husband; to win the silent comparison with other women at gatherings.
3. **"اشتري شي يفرق"** (buy something that actually works) → To stop **wasting money** on products that disappoint; to finally find a brand that delivers on promises.
4. **"شي طبيعي ومضمون"** (something natural and safe) → To **protect herself** from skin damage, hair loss, and shady ingredients she's read horror stories about online.
5. **"شي يخليني افتخر بنفسي"** (something that makes me proud of myself) → **Self-respect** through visible self-care; this is therapy disguised as shopping.

## Her objections (every one of these must be neutralized in copy)

1. **"غالي"** (Expensive) → reframe as "investment that pays off vs. the cost of staying invisible." Compare to one café visit, one dress.
2. **"ما يطلع زي الصورة"** (It won't be like the photo) → show real before/after, real Kuwaiti customers, video reviews.
3. **"خوفي يكون فيك"** (Afraid it's fake) → show ingredient sources, certifications, brand heritage (Nature's Bounty = American, Sakura = Japanese), batch numbers.
4. **"ما يوصل أو يتأخر"** (Won't arrive / will be late) → COD = "you don't pay until it's in your hand." Same-day or next-day Kuwait delivery.
5. **"ما عندي وقت أرجع لو ما عجبني"** (I don't have time to return) → satisfaction guarantee, easy return language, customer service WhatsApp.
6. **"شلون أدفع؟"** (How do I pay?) → COD only, no card needed, no commitment.
7. **"خوفي من المكونات"** (Worried about ingredients) → transparent ingredient list, dermatologically tested, no harmful chemicals callouts.
8. **"جربت أشياء أحسن وما نفعت"** (I've tried better and it didn't work) → "this works **because** of X (specific mechanism), not because we say so."
9. **"حسابي ع الانستا/سناب صار يعرض إعلانات وايد"** (I'm tired of social ads) → social proof from real local women, not influencers.
10. **"بطلبه ويوصل وأهلي يشوفونه"** (Embarrassed family will see the package) → discreet packaging mention.

## Cultural / linguistic nuances to encode

- Use **چ (ch)** for second-person feminine forms: `بشرتچ، شعرچ، عمرچ، حبيبتچ`.
- Use **وايد** (a lot) instead of MSA `كثيراً`.
- Use **چنه/چنها** sparingly — only when natural.
- Greetings: `هلا فيچ`, `الله يحفظچ`, `يعطيچ العافية`.
- Mentioning **العين** / evil eye is culturally important — frame results modestly: "بدون ما تلفتين الانتباه بطريقة سيئة" — but never overdo it.
- Religious modesty: avoid suggestive imagery, avoid showing too much skin in lifestyle shots.
- Family-positive: husband / mother / sister mentions resonate ("شعري رجع يعجب زوجي").
- Time of year matters: Ramadan ("جاهزة للعيد"), summer heat ("جو الكويت قاتل")، school season, wedding season (winter).

## What "they trust" (proof types ranked)

1. **Ingredient name + country of origin** ("كولاجين بحري ياباني" / "نتشرز باونتي الأمريكية")
2. **Real local customer photos + testimonials** (Kuwaiti accent in voice/text)
3. **Number-based claims with a study/source** ("87% من المستخدمات لاحظن نتايج بعد 14 يوم")
4. **Certifications** (FDA, KSA SFDA mention, dermatologically tested badges)
5. **Influencer / known person endorsement** (lower in trust now — algorithm-fatigued)
6. **Press / media logos** ("ظهرنا في…")

## Buying triggers (when she pulls the trigger)

- A specific event coming up (wedding, family gathering, trip, anniversary)
- A bad photo of herself she just saw
- A friend commented on her appearance (positively or negatively)
- Post-pregnancy / post-illness recovery moment
- "Self-reward" moment (got salary, finished a hard week)
- Seasonal: start of Ramadan, end of school year, summer prep

## What NOT to do (instant ICP turn-offs)

- Don't use Egyptian or Lebanese dialect.
- Don't use male model voiceover or imagery in videos.
- Don't use comparative shaming ("if you don't, you'll look like…").
- Don't claim medical results without disclaimer.
- Don't run "limited to 100 customers" obvious-fake scarcity (she sees through it).
- Don't ask for email at checkout (she rarely uses it).
- Don't ask for "address" before phone — phone is the trust anchor.
- Don't auto-play sound on the site.
